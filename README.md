# 🛢️ 2026 Strait of Hormuz Oil Crisis — Verified BI Analytics Dashboard

An interactive, fully-sourced business intelligence dashboard analyzing the ongoing 2026 Strait of Hormuz energy crisis and its impact on Iran, Muslim-majority OPEC+ nations, and global oil markets.

## 📊 What this project covers

- **Crisis overview** — Real-time Brent crude price trajectory, global supply gap analysis, and bypass infrastructure capacity (Saudi East-West Pipeline, UAE Fujairah route, IEA reserve release)
- **Iran economy** — Verified crude production history (2018–Jan 2026), export revenue ($43B in FY2024), and China trade dependency
- **Muslim-majority producers** — Country-by-country impact analysis across Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Iraq, Kuwait, Iran, Oman, and Pakistan
- **Crisis timeline** — Fully sourced event log from June 2025 escalation through March 2026
- **Historical comparison** — 2026 vs. 1973, 1979, 1980, and 1990 oil crises

## 📌 Key findings

- 20M barrels/day disrupted — largest supply shock since 1973 Arab embargo
- Brent crude surged from $73/bbl (late Feb) to $126/bbl peak
- Saudi Arabia (5.5M bbl/d = 38% of Hormuz crude) and UAE diverting via bypass pipelines
- Iran — despite being the belligerent — continues exporting ~2.1M bbl/day to China
- Pakistan and Bangladesh face critical LNG shortages (~99% and ~72% Gulf-dependent)
- Dallas Fed estimates global GDP hit of −2.9pp annualised in Q2 2026
