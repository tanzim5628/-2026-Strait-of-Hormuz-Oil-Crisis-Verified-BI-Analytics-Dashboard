US crude imports from Persian Gulf (2024)
Source: U.S. EIA — 0.5 mb/d from Persian Gulf = 7% of total US crude imports = 2% of US petroleum consumption
The US is relatively insulated: Persian Gulf crude accounts for only 2% of US petroleum consumption (EIA). The crisis primarily hits Asia (84% of Hormuz flows), not Western markets directly. However, global oil price spikes affect US pump prices regardless of source.
Verified crisis timeline — all events sourced
Source: Wikipedia · NPR · CNBC · Kpler · CRS · Dallas Fed
Jun 2025
12-day US-Israel air conflict with Iran (prior escalation). Iran does not close strait. War-risk premiums begin climbing.
Source: Wikipedia / CNBC
Oct 2025
US revokes Chabahar Port sanctions waiver. Treasury letter confirms. Iran faces renewed maximum pressure.
Source: CRS R45281
15–20 Feb 2026
Iran surges oil exports to 3× normal rate to reduce storage risk ahead of expected conflict. Saudi Arabia does the same. War-risk insurance premiums rise from 0.125% to 0.2%–0.4% of ship value per transit.
Source: Wikipedia (confirmed)
28 Feb 2026
US and Israel launch joint military strikes on Iran, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei. Iran retaliates with missile and drone attacks on US bases, Israeli territory, UAE (Jebel Ali port, Abu Dhabi), Saudi Arabia, and Bahrain. IRGC issues warnings prohibiting vessel passage through the strait.
Source: Wikipedia · Kpler (Mar 1)
1–4 Mar 2026
Tanker traffic drops ~70%. 150+ ships anchor outside strait. Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd suspend Mideast routes. Brent crosses $100/bbl on 8 Mar. Iraq and Kuwait begin curtailing production — storage full, no export route.
Source: Wikipedia · NPR · Dallas Fed
3 Mar 2026
President Trump orders US DFC to provide political risk insurance and guarantees for maritime energy trade, up to ~$20B on a rolling basis.
Source: CRS R45281
5 Mar 2026
IRGC announces Hormuz closed only to US, Israel, and Western allies. Confirmed again on 8 March. Iranian and Chinese-flagged ships continue limited transit.
Source: Wikipedia (confirmed)
10 Mar 2026
Saudi Arabia increasingly diverts oil via East-West Crude Oil Pipeline to Red Sea port of Yanbu. UAE diverts via Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline to Fujairah on Arabian Sea. IEA announces unprecedented release of 400M barrels from strategic reserves.
Source: Wikipedia (confirmed)
12 Mar 2026
Iran has made 21 confirmed attacks on merchant ships. Turkish ship approved passage (13 March). Two Indian-flagged gas carriers and a Saudi tanker with 1M barrels for India allowed through. Drone strikes hit Oman's Duqm and Salalah bypass ports — at least one fuel storage tank damaged.
Source: Wikipedia (confirmed) · Joint War Committee adds Oman to high-risk list
Mid-Mar 2026
Brent peaks at $126/bbl. Oil prices risen from $73/bbl (late Feb) to $103/bbl by mid-March (Kpler/LNRG). EU gas prices up 63% week-on-week. Fertilizer prices: urea from $475 to $680/metric ton. Aluminum markets disrupted (Middle East = ~21% of 2025 global supply).
Source: Wikipedia · CNBC · LNRG Technology · Dallas Fed
25 Mar 2026 (today)
Crisis ongoing. Dallas Fed model: full closure removing ~20% of global oil supplies expected to lower global real GDP growth by annualised 2.9pp in Q2 2026 and raise WTI to $98/bbl average. Iran continues exporting ~2.1mb/d to China. Duration of closure remains uncertain.
Source: Dallas Fed (Mar 20, 2026) · CRS R45281
